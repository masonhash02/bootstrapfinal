Inside a Modern F1 Car

Main Body Components

  • Monocoque: The carbon fiber “survival cell” that forms the core chassis, protecting the driver and anchoring all other components.
  • Sidepods: House radiators and channel airflow for engine and electronics cooling.
  • Coke Panel: The narrow waist area behind the sidepods, shaped for optimal airflow to the rear of the car.

Aerodynamics

  • Front Wing and Nose: Directs air under and around the car, generating front-end downforce and shaping the airflow for the rest of the car.
  • Rear Wing: Creates rear downforce for stability. Features the DRS (Drag Reduction System) flap for overtaking.
  • Floor and Floor Fences: The floor is critical for downforce, using ground effect to “suck” the car to the track. Fences help manage airflow under the car.
  • Diffuser: Expands airflow at the rear, increasing downforce by accelerating air under the car and reducing pressure.

Brake Ducts

  • Front and Rear Brake Ducts: Channel cooling air to the carbon brakes, which can reach 1,000°C (1,830°F). Ducts are also shaped for aerodynamic benefit.

Suspension

  • Double Wishbone Suspension: Both front and rear use lightweight, aerodynamic wishbones for precise handling and minimal drag.
  • Pushrod/Pullrod: Different teams use either system to connect the suspension to the chassis, affecting car balance and aerodynamics.

Power Unit & Energy Systems

  • Engine: 1.6L V6 turbocharged hybrid, producing up to 950 hp. The engine sits behind the driver and is paired with a large turbocharger.
  • Energy Recovery System (ERS): Includes the MGU-K (harvests braking energy) and MGU-H (harvests heat from the turbo). Stores energy in a battery to provide an extra 160 hp boost for overtaking and acceleration.

DRS (Drag Reduction System)

The DRS is a movable flap in the rear wing that the driver can open in designated zones to reduce drag and increase top speed for overtaking. It’s only available when within one second of the car ahead during a race, and is controlled by a button on the steering wheel.

Other Key Features

  • Halo: A titanium safety device that protects the driver’s head from debris and impacts.
  • Transmission: 8-speed semi-automatic gearbox, paddle-shifted from the steering wheel.
  • Wheels and Tyres: 18-inch wheels with slick tyres for dry and treaded for wet, all designed for maximum grip and performance.
  • Brakes: Carbon discs and pads, extremely powerful and heat-resistant.
