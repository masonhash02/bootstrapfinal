Monaco Grand Prix

The Monaco Grand Prix is the most glamorous and iconic street race in Formula 1. Its tight, twisting layout through the streets of Monte Carlo demands absolute precision and skill.

  • First Grand Prix: 1929 (F1 since 1950)
  • Location: Monte Carlo, Monaco
  • Lap Length: 3.337 km
  • Laps: 78
  • Notable Features: Tunnel, Fairmont Hairpin, Casino Square

Silverstone Circuit

Known as the "Home of British Motorsport," Silverstone hosted the first ever F1 World Championship race in 1950. The circuit is famous for its high-speed corners and passionate fans.

  • First Grand Prix: 1948 (F1 since 1950)
  • Location: Silverstone, United Kingdom
  • Lap Length: 5.891 km
  • Laps: 52
  • Notable Features: Maggotts-Becketts, Copse, Hangar Straight

Spa-Francorchamps

Spa is a favorite among drivers and fans for its sweeping corners, dramatic elevation changes, and unpredictable weather. It is home to the legendary Eau Rouge and Raidillon.

  • First Grand Prix: 1925 (F1 since 1950)
  • Location: Stavelot, Belgium
  • Lap Length: 7.004 km
  • Laps: 44
  • Notable Features: Eau Rouge, Raidillon, La Source
